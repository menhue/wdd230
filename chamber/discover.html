Overview

  • Gbarpolu County, located in the northern part of Liberia,
  • is one of the nation's newest counties, having been created out of Lofa County.
  • It is often referred to as Liberia's "breadbasket" due to its significant contribution to the country's food production.
  • The county is renowned for its fertile lands, which support agricultural activities that sustain local communities and supply food to other parts of Liberia.
  • The county has an estimated population of about 1 million people, most of whom are indigenous Liberians. Its agricultural strength and cultural heritage make it an important region within the country.

Life

The lifestyle of the people in Gbarpolu is predominantly centered around agriculture. Most residents are agriculturists who focus on farming as their primary livelihood, contributing significantly to the region's reputation as Liberia's "breadbasket."

Economy

Gbarpolu plays a significant role in Liberia's economy through its agricultural industry. The county contributes approximately $45 million annually to the nation's economy, underscoring its importance as a major agricultural hub.
